Compute the following cross product:

$\, (-5,-9,-3)\, \times \, (-6,10,-10)\,$

\hrule

1. Construct a matrix where the first row contains unit vectors $\hat{\text{i}}, \hat{\text{j}},\text{ and }\hat{\text{k}};\text{ and the second and third rows are made of vectors }\, (-5,-9,-3)\,\text{  and }\, (-6,10,-10)\,$ :

$\left(
\begin{array}{ccc}
\hat{\text{i}} & \hat{\text{j}} & \hat{\text{k}} \\
 -5 & -9 & -3 \\
 -6 & 10 & -10 \\
\end{array}
\right)$

2. Take the determinant of this matrix:

$\left| 
\begin{array}{ccc}
\hat{\text{i}} & \hat{\text{j}} & \hat{\text{k}} \\
 -5 & -9 & -3 \\
 -6 & 10 & -10 \\
\end{array}
\right|$

3. Expand with respect to row $1:$

= $\left| 
\begin{array}{ccc}
\hat{\text{i}} & \hat{\text{j}} & \hat{\text{k}} \\
 -5 & -9 & -3 \\
 -6 & 10 & -10 \\
\end{array}
\right|$

4. The determinant of the matrix $\left(
\begin{array}{ccc}
\hat{\text{i}} & \hat{\text{j}} & \hat{\text{k}} \\
 -5 & -9 & -3 \\
 -6 & 10 & -10 \\
\end{array}
\right)\text{ is given by }\hat{\text{i}} \left| 
\begin{array}{cc}
-9 & -3 \\
 10 & -10 \\
\end{array}
\right| +\left(-\hat{\text{j}}\right) \left| 
\begin{array}{cc}
-5 & -3 \\
 -6 & -10 \\
\end{array}
\right| +\hat{\text{k}} \left| 
\begin{array}{cc}
-5 & -9 \\
 -6 & 10 \\
\end{array}
\right|$ :

= $\hat{\text{i}} \left| 
\begin{array}{cc}
-9 & -3 \\
 10 & -10 \\
\end{array}
\right| +\left(-\hat{\text{j}}\right) \left| 
\begin{array}{cc}
-5 & -3 \\
 -6 & -10 \\
\end{array}
\right| +\hat{\text{k}} \left| 
\begin{array}{cc}
-5 & -9 \\
 -6 & 10 \\
\end{array}
\right|$

5. $\hat{\text{i}} \left| 
\begin{array}{cc}
-9 & -3 \\
 10 & -10 \\
\end{array}
\right| =\hat{\text{i}} ((-9)\, (-10)-(-3)\, \times \, 10)=\hat{\text{i}} 120=120 \hat{\text{i}}$:

= $120 \hat{\text{i}}+\left(-\hat{\text{j}}\right) \left| 
\begin{array}{cc}
-5 & -3 \\
 -6 & -10 \\
\end{array}
\right| +\hat{\text{k}} \left| 
\begin{array}{cc}
-5 & -9 \\
 -6 & 10 \\
\end{array}
\right|$

6. $-\hat{\text{j}} \left| 
\begin{array}{cc}
-5 & -3 \\
 -6 & -10 \\
\end{array}
\right| =-\hat{\text{j}} ((-5)\, (-10)-(-3)\, (-6))=-\hat{\text{j}} 32=-32 \hat{\text{j}}$:

= $120 \hat{\text{i}}+-32 \hat{\text{j}}+\hat{\text{k}} \left| 
\begin{array}{cc}
-5 & -9 \\
 -6 & 10 \\
\end{array}
\right|$

7. $\hat{\text{k}} \left| 
\begin{array}{cc}
-5 & -9 \\
 -6 & 10 \\
\end{array}
\right| =\hat{\text{k}} ((-5)\, \times \, 10-(-9)\, (-6))=\hat{\text{k}} (-104)=-104 \hat{\text{k}}$:

= $120 \hat{\text{i}}-32 \hat{\text{j}}+-104 \hat{\text{k}}$

8. Order the terms in a more natural way:

= $120 \hat{\text{i}}-32 \hat{\text{j}}-104 \hat{\text{k}}$

9. $120 \hat{\text{i}}-32 \hat{\text{j}}-104 \hat{\text{k}}=\, (120,-32,-104)\,$ :

Answer: $\, (120,-32,-104)\,$
