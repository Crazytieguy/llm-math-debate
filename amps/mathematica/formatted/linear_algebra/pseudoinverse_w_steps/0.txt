Find the inverse:

$\left(
\begin{array}{cc}
-1 & 4 \\
 2 & -5 \\
\end{array}
\right)^{-1}$

\hrule

1. Using a formula for the inverse of a 2$\times $2 matrix, $\left(
\begin{array}{cc}
-1 & 4 \\
 2 & -5 \\
\end{array}
\right)^{-1}=\frac{1}{-(-5)-2\times 4}\left(
\begin{array}{cc}
-5 & -4 \\
 -2 & -1 \\
\end{array}
\right):$

$\frac{1}{-(-5)-2\times 4}\left(
\begin{array}{cc}
-5 & -4 \\
 -2 & -1 \\
\end{array}
\right)$

2. Simplify: $\frac{1}{-(-5)-2\times 4}=-\frac{1}{3}$:

Answer: $-\frac{1}{3}\left(
\begin{array}{cc}
-5 & -4 \\
 -2 & -1 \\
\end{array}
\right)$
