Find the rank of the matrix:

$M=\left(
\begin{array}{cc}
-9 & -2 \\
 0 & 6 \\
 6 & 4 \\
\end{array}
\right)$

\hrule

1. Reduce matrix $M$ to row echelon form:

$\left(
\begin{array}{cc}
-9 & -2 \\
 0 & 6 \\
 6 & 4 \\
\end{array}
\right)$

2. Add $\frac{2}{3}\, \times \,\text{ (row }1)\text{ to row }3:$

$\left(
\begin{array}{cc}
-9 & -2 \\
 0 & 6 \\
 0 & \frac{8}{3} \\
\end{array}
\right)$

3. Subtract $\frac{4}{9}\, \times \,\text{ (row }2)\text{ from row }3:$

$\left(
\begin{array}{cc}
-9 & -2 \\
 0 & 6 \\
 0 & 0 \\
\end{array}
\right)$

4. There are $2\text{ nonzero rows in the row echelon form of the matrix, so the rank is }2:$

Answer: rank$\left(
\begin{array}{cc}
-9 & -2 \\
 0 & 6 \\
 6 & 4 \\
\end{array}
\right) =\text{ rank}\left(
\begin{array}{cc}
-9 & -2 \\
 0 & 6 \\
 0 & 0 \\
\end{array}
\right)=2$
