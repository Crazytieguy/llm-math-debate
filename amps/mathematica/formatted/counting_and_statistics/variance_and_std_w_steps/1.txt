Find the (sample) variance of the list:

$(8,-6,-13)$

\hrule

1. The (sample) variance of a list of numbers $X=\left\{X_1,X_2,\ldots ,X_n\right\}\text{ with mean }\mu =\frac{X_1+X_2+\ldots +X_n}{n}$ is given by:

$\frac{\left| X_1-\mu \right| {}^2+\left| X_2-\mu \right| {}^2+\ldots +\left| X_n-\mu \right| {}^2}{n-1}$

2. There are $n=3\text{ elements in the list }X=\{8,-6,-13\}$:

$\frac{\left| X_1-\mu \right| {}^2+\left| X_2-\mu \right| {}^2+\left| X_3-\mu \right| {}^2}{3-1}$

3. $\begin{array}{l}
\text{The elements }X_i\text{ of the list }X=\{8,-6,-13\}\text{ are: }\\
 
\begin{array}{l}
X_1=8 \\
 X_2=-6 \\
 X_3=-13 \\
\end{array}
 \\
\end{array}$

$\frac{| 8-\mu | ^2+| -\mu -6| ^2+| -\mu -13| ^2}{3-1}$

4. The mean $(\mu )\text{ is given by$\backslash $n$\backslash $n}\mu =\frac{X_1+X_2+X_3}{3}=\frac{8-6-13}{3}=-\frac{11}{3}$:

$\frac{\left| 8-\left(-\frac{11}{3}\right)\right| ^2+\left| -\frac{-11}{3}-6\right| ^2+\left| -\frac{-11}{3}-13\right| ^2}{3-1}$

5. $\begin{array}{l}
\text{The values of the differences are: }\\
 
\begin{array}{l}
-\frac{-11}{3}+8=\frac{35}{3} \\
 -\frac{-11}{3}-6=-\frac{7}{3} \\
 -\frac{-11}{3}-13=-\frac{28}{3} \\
 3-1=2 \\
\end{array}
 \\
\end{array}$

$\frac{\left| \frac{35}{3}\right| ^2+\left| -\frac{7}{3}\right| ^2+\left| -\frac{28}{3}\right| ^2}{2}$

6. $\begin{array}{l}
\text{The values of the terms in the numerator are}: \\
 
\begin{array}{l}
\left| \frac{35}{3}\right| ^2 = \frac{1225}{9} \\
 \left| -\frac{7}{3}\right| ^2 = \frac{49}{9} \\
 \left| -\frac{28}{3}\right| ^2 = \frac{784}{9} \\
\end{array}
 \\
\end{array}$

$\frac{\frac{1225}{9}+\frac{49}{9}+\frac{784}{9}}{2}$

7. $\frac{1225}{9}+\frac{49}{9}+\frac{784}{9}=\frac{686}{3}$:

$\frac{\frac{686}{3}}{2}$

8. $\frac{\frac{686}{3}}{2}=\frac{343}{3}$:

Answer: $\frac{343}{3}$
