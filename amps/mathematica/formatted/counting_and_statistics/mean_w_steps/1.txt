Find the mean of the list:

$(-8 \log (2),-\log (2),-8 \log (2),-13 \log (2),-14 \log (2))$

\hrule

1. The mean of a list of numbers is given by:

$\frac{(\text{sum of elements})}{(\text{number of elements})}$

2. $(\text{sum of elements}) = -8 \log (2)-\log (2)-8 \log (2)-13 \log (2)-14 \log (2):$

$\frac{-8 \log (2)-\log (2)-8 \log (2)-13 \log (2)-14 \log (2)}{(\text{number of elements})}$

3. Counting, we see that the list has $5$ elements:

$\frac{-8 \log (2)-\log (2)-8 \log (2)-13 \log (2)-14 \log (2)}{5}$

4. $-8 \log (2)-\log (2)-8 \log (2)-13 \log (2)-14 \log (2) = -44 \log (2):$

Answer: $\frac{1}{5} (-44 \log (2))$
