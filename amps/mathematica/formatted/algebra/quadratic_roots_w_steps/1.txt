$15 x^2+6 x-10=0$

\hrule

1. Divide both sides by $15:$

$x^2+\frac{2 x}{5}-\frac{2}{3}=0$

2. Add $\frac{2}{3}$ to both sides:

$x^2+\frac{2 x}{5}=\frac{2}{3}$

3. Add $\frac{1}{25}$ to both sides:

$x^2+\frac{2 x}{5}+\frac{1}{25}=\frac{53}{75}$

4. Write the left hand side as a square:

$\left(x+\frac{1}{5}\right)^2=\frac{53}{75}$

5. Take the square root of both sides:

$x+\frac{1}{5}=\frac{\sqrt{\frac{53}{3}}}{5}\text{ or }x+\frac{1}{5}=-\frac{\sqrt{\frac{53}{3}}}{5}$

6. Subtract $\frac{1}{5}$ from both sides:

$x=\frac{\sqrt{\frac{53}{3}}}{5}-\frac{1}{5}\text{ or }x+\frac{1}{5}=-\frac{\sqrt{\frac{53}{3}}}{5}$

7. Subtract $\frac{1}{5}$ from both sides:

Answer: $x=\frac{\sqrt{\frac{53}{3}}}{5}-\frac{1}{5}\text{ or }x=-\frac{1}{5}-\frac{\sqrt{\frac{53}{3}}}{5}$
