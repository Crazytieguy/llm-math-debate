Problem:
Given the equation $4 x^2-3 x-10 y^2+10 y+4=0$, complete the square.
Answer:
\begin{array}{l}
 
\begin{array}{l}
 \text{Complete the square}: \\
 -10 y^2+10 y+4 x^2-3 x+4=0 \\
\end{array}
 \\
\hline
 
\begin{array}{l}
 \text{Subtract }4 \text{from }\text{both }\text{sides}: \\
 -10 y^2+10 y+4 x^2-3 x=-4 \\
\end{array}
 \\
 
\begin{array}{l}
 \text{Group }\text{terms }\text{with }x \text{and }y \text{separately, }\text{leaving }\text{placeholder }\text{constants}: \\
 \left(4 x^2-3 x+\underline{\text{   }}\right)+\left(-10 y^2+10 y+\underline{\text{   }}\right)=\underline{\text{   }}-4 \\
\end{array}
 \\
 
\begin{array}{l}
 \left(4 x^2-3 x+\underline{\text{   }}\right)=4 \left(x^2-\frac{3 x}{4}+\underline{\text{   }}\right): \\
 \fbox{$4 \left(x^2-\frac{3 x}{4}+\underline{\text{   }}\right)$}+\left(-10 y^2+10 y+\underline{\text{   }}\right)=\underline{\text{   }}-4 \\
\end{array}
 \\
 
\begin{array}{l}
 \left(-10 y^2+10 y+\underline{\text{   }}\right)=-10 \left(y^2-y+\underline{\text{   }}\right): \\
 4 \left(x^2-\frac{3 x}{4}+\underline{\text{   }}\right)+\fbox{$-10 \left(y^2-y+\underline{\text{   }}\right)$}=\underline{\text{   }}-4 \\
\end{array}
 \\
 
\begin{array}{l}
 
\begin{array}{l}
 \text{Take }\text{one }\text{half }\text{of }\text{the }\text{coefficient }\text{of }x \text{and }\text{square }\text{it. }\text{Then }\text{add }\text{it }\text{to }\text{both }\text{sides }\text{of }\text{the }\text{equation, }\text{multiplying }\text{by }\text{the }\text{factored }\text{constant }4 \text{on }\text{the }\text{right.} \\
 \text{Insert }\left(\frac{\frac{-3}{4}}{2}\right)^2=\frac{9}{64} \text{on }\text{the }\text{left }\text{and }4\times \frac{9}{64}=\frac{9}{16} \text{on }\text{the }\text{right}: \\
\end{array}
 \\
 4 \left(x^2-\frac{3 x}{4}+\fbox{$\frac{9}{64}$}\right)-10 \left(y^2-y+\underline{\text{   }}\right)=\fbox{$\frac{9}{16}$}-4 \\
\end{array}
 \\
 
\begin{array}{l}
 \frac{9}{16}-4=-\frac{55}{16}: \\
 4 \left(x^2-\frac{3 x}{4}+\frac{9}{64}\right)-10 \left(y^2-y+\underline{\text{   }}\right)=\fbox{$-\frac{55}{16}$} \\
\end{array}
 \\
 
\begin{array}{l}
 
\begin{array}{l}
 \text{Take }\text{one }\text{half }\text{of }\text{the }\text{coefficient }\text{of }y \text{and }\text{square }\text{it. }\text{Then }\text{add }\text{it }\text{to }\text{both }\text{sides }\text{of }\text{the }\text{equation, }\text{multiplying }\text{by }\text{the }\text{factored }\text{constant }-10 \text{on }\text{the }\text{right.} \\
 \text{Insert }\left(\frac{-1}{2}\right)^2=\frac{1}{4} \text{on }\text{the }\text{left }\text{and }\frac{-10}{4}=-\frac{5}{2} \text{on }\text{the }\text{right}: \\
\end{array}
 \\
 4 \left(x^2-\frac{3 x}{4}+\frac{9}{64}\right)-10 \left(y^2-y+\fbox{$\frac{1}{4}$}\right)=\fbox{$-\frac{5}{2}$}-\frac{55}{16} \\
\end{array}
 \\
 
\begin{array}{l}
 -\frac{55}{16}-\frac{5}{2}=-\frac{95}{16}: \\
 4 \left(x^2-\frac{3 x}{4}+\frac{9}{64}\right)-10 \left(y^2-y+\frac{1}{4}\right)=\fbox{$-\frac{95}{16}$} \\
\end{array}
 \\
 
\begin{array}{l}
 x^2-\frac{3 x}{4}+\frac{9}{64}=\left(x-\frac{3}{8}\right)^2: \\
 4 \fbox{$\left(x-\frac{3}{8}\right)^2$}-10 \left(y^2-y+\frac{1}{4}\right)=-\frac{95}{16} \\
\end{array}
 \\
 
\begin{array}{l}
 y^2-y+\frac{1}{4}=\left(y-\frac{1}{2}\right)^2: \\
 \fbox{$
\begin{array}{ll}
 \text{Answer:} &  \\
 \text{} & 4 \left(x-\frac{3}{8}\right)^2-\text{10 }\fbox{$\left(y-\frac{1}{2}\right)^2$}=-\frac{95}{16} \\
\end{array}
$} \\
\end{array}
 \\
\end{array}
