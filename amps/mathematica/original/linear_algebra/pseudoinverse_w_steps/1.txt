Problem:
Compute the (pseudo)inverse of
$\left(
\begin{array}{ccc}
 1 & 1 & 3 \\
 0 & 3 & 1 \\
 -4 & -4 & -1 \\
\end{array}
\right)$.
Answer:
\begin{array}{l}
 
\begin{array}{l}
 \text{Find the inverse}: \\
 \left(
\begin{array}{ccc}
 1 & 1 & 3 \\
 0 & 3 & 1 \\
 -4 & -4 & -1 \\
\end{array}
\right)^{-1} \\
\end{array}
 \\
\hline
 
\begin{array}{l}
 \text{To }\text{find }\text{the }\text{inverse, }\text{augment }\text{the }\text{given }\text{matrix }\text{with }\text{the }\text{identity }\text{matrix }\text{and }\text{perform }\text{Gaussian }\text{elimination}: \\
 \left(
\begin{array}{ccc|ccc}
 1 & 1 & 3 & 1 & 0 & 0 \\
 0 & 3 & 1 & 0 & 1 & 0 \\
 -4 & -4 & -1 & 0 & 0 & 1 \\
\end{array}
\right) \\
\end{array}
 \\
 
\begin{array}{l}
 \text{Add }4\, \times \, \text{(row }1) \text{to }\text{row }3: \\
 \left(
\begin{array}{ccc|ccc}
 1 & 1 & 3 & 1 & 0 & 0 \\
 0 & 3 & 1 & 0 & 1 & 0 \\
 0 & 0 & 11 & 4 & 0 & 1 \\
\end{array}
\right) \\
\end{array}
 \\
 
\begin{array}{l}
 \text{Divide }\text{row }3 \text{by }11: \\
 \left(
\begin{array}{ccc|ccc}
 1 & 1 & 3 & 1 & 0 & 0 \\
 0 & 3 & 1 & 0 & 1 & 0 \\
 0 & 0 & 1 & \frac{4}{11} & 0 & \frac{1}{11} \\
\end{array}
\right) \\
\end{array}
 \\
 
\begin{array}{l}
 \text{Subtract }\text{row }3 \text{from }\text{row }2: \\
 \left(
\begin{array}{ccc|ccc}
 1 & 1 & 3 & 1 & 0 & 0 \\
 0 & 3 & 0 & -\frac{4}{11} & 1 & -\frac{1}{11} \\
 0 & 0 & 1 & \frac{4}{11} & 0 & \frac{1}{11} \\
\end{array}
\right) \\
\end{array}
 \\
 
\begin{array}{l}
 \text{Subtract }3\, \times \, \text{(row }3) \text{from }\text{row }1: \\
 \left(
\begin{array}{ccc|ccc}
 1 & 1 & 0 & -\frac{1}{11} & 0 & -\frac{3}{11} \\
 0 & 3 & 0 & -\frac{4}{11} & 1 & -\frac{1}{11} \\
 0 & 0 & 1 & \frac{4}{11} & 0 & \frac{1}{11} \\
\end{array}
\right) \\
\end{array}
 \\
 
\begin{array}{l}
 \text{Divide }\text{row }2 \text{by }3: \\
 \left(
\begin{array}{ccc|ccc}
 1 & 1 & 0 & -\frac{1}{11} & 0 & -\frac{3}{11} \\
 0 & 1 & 0 & -\frac{4}{33} & \frac{1}{3} & -\frac{1}{33} \\
 0 & 0 & 1 & \frac{4}{11} & 0 & \frac{1}{11} \\
\end{array}
\right) \\
\end{array}
 \\
 
\begin{array}{l}
 \text{Subtract }\text{row }2 \text{from }\text{row }1: \\
 \left(
\begin{array}{ccc|ccc}
 1 & 0 & 0 & \frac{1}{33} & -\frac{1}{3} & -\frac{8}{33} \\
 0 & 1 & 0 & -\frac{4}{33} & \frac{1}{3} & -\frac{1}{33} \\
 0 & 0 & 1 & \frac{4}{11} & 0 & \frac{1}{11} \\
\end{array}
\right) \\
\end{array}
 \\
 
\begin{array}{l}
 \text{Read }\text{off }\text{the }\text{inverse}: \\
 \left(
\begin{array}{ccc}
 1 & 1 & 3 \\
 0 & 3 & 1 \\
 -4 & -4 & -1 \\
\end{array}
\right)^{-1}=\left(
\begin{array}{ccc}
 \frac{1}{33} & -\frac{1}{3} & -\frac{8}{33} \\
 -\frac{4}{33} & \frac{1}{3} & -\frac{1}{33} \\
 \frac{4}{11} & 0 & \frac{1}{11} \\
\end{array}
\right) \\
\end{array}
 \\
 
\begin{array}{l}
 \text{Simplify }\text{by }\text{factoring }\text{out }\text{an }\text{appropriate }\text{term}: \\
 \fbox{$
\begin{array}{ll}
 \text{Answer:} &  \\
 \text{} & \frac{1}{33}\left(
\begin{array}{ccc}
 1 & -11 & -8 \\
 -4 & 11 & -1 \\
 12 & 0 & 3 \\
\end{array}
\right) \\
\end{array}
$} \\
\end{array}
 \\
\end{array}
