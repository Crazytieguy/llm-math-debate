Given the following problem:

```

Find all the eigenvalues of the matrix $M:$

$M=\left(
\begin{array}{cc}
-1 & 0 \\
 4 & -9 \\
\end{array}
\right)$

```

And correct solution:

```

1. Find $\lambda \in \mathbb{C}\text{ such that }M v=\lambda  v\text{ for some nonzero vector }v:$

$M v=\lambda  v$

2. Rewrite the equation $M v=\lambda  v\text{ as }(M-\mathbb{I} \lambda ) v=0:$

$(M-\mathbb{I} \lambda ) v=0$

3. Find all $\lambda\text{  such that }| M-\mathbb{I} \lambda | =0:$

$| M-\mathbb{I} \lambda | =0$

4. $\begin{array}{lll}
M-\mathbb{I} \lambda  & = & \left(
\begin{array}{cc}
-1 & 0 \\
 4 & -9 \\
\end{array}
\right)-\left(
\begin{array}{cc}
1 & 0 \\
 0 & 1 \\
\end{array}
\right) \lambda  \\
\text{  }& = & \left(
\begin{array}{cc}
-\lambda -1 & 0 \\
 4 & -\lambda -9 \\
\end{array}
\right) \\
\end{array}$

$\left| 
\begin{array}{cc}
-\lambda -1 & 0 \\
 4 & -\lambda -9 \\
\end{array}
\right| =0$

5. $\left| 
\begin{array}{cc}
-\lambda -1 & 0 \\
 4 & -\lambda -9 \\
\end{array}
\right| =\lambda ^2+10 \lambda +9:$

$\lambda ^2+10 \lambda +9=0$

6. The left hand side factors into a product with two terms:

$(\lambda +1) (\lambda +9)=0$

7. Split into two equations:

$\lambda +1=0\text{ or }\lambda +9=0$

8. Subtract $1$ from both sides:

$\lambda =-1\text{ or }\lambda +9=0$

9. Subtract $9$ from both sides:

Answer: $\lambda =-1\text{ or }\lambda =-9$

```

Insert an error into step 9.
