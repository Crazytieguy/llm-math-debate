Given the following problem:

```

Find all the eigenvalues of the matrix $M:$

$M=\left(
\begin{array}{cc}
-1 & -7 \\
 6 & -5 \\
\end{array}
\right)$

```

And correct solution:

```

1. Find $\lambda \in \mathbb{C}\text{ such that }M v=\lambda  v\text{ for some nonzero vector }v:$

$M v=\lambda  v$

2. Rewrite the equation $M v=\lambda  v\text{ as }(M-\mathbb{I} \lambda ) v=0:$

$(M-\mathbb{I} \lambda ) v=0$

3. Find all $\lambda\text{  such that }| M-\mathbb{I} \lambda | =0:$

$| M-\mathbb{I} \lambda | =0$

4. $\begin{array}{lll}
M-\mathbb{I} \lambda  & = & \left(
\begin{array}{cc}
-1 & -7 \\
 6 & -5 \\
\end{array}
\right)-\left(
\begin{array}{cc}
1 & 0 \\
 0 & 1 \\
\end{array}
\right) \lambda  \\
\text{  }& = & \left(
\begin{array}{cc}
-\lambda -1 & -7 \\
 6 & -\lambda -5 \\
\end{array}
\right) \\
\end{array}$

$\left| 
\begin{array}{cc}
-\lambda -1 & -7 \\
 6 & -\lambda -5 \\
\end{array}
\right| =0$

5. $\left| 
\begin{array}{cc}
-\lambda -1 & -7 \\
 6 & -\lambda -5 \\
\end{array}
\right| =\lambda ^2+6 \lambda +47:$

$\lambda ^2+6 \lambda +47=0$

6. Subtract 47 from both sides:

$\lambda ^2+6 \lambda =-47$

7. Add $9$ to both sides:

$\lambda ^2+6 \lambda +9=-38$

8. Write the left hand side as a square:

$(\lambda +3)^2=-38$

9. Take the square root of both sides:

$\lambda +3=i \sqrt{38}\text{ or }\lambda +3=-i \sqrt{38}$

10. Subtract $3$ from both sides:

$\lambda =i \sqrt{38}-3\text{ or }\lambda +3=-i \sqrt{38}$

11. Subtract $3$ from both sides:

Answer: $\lambda =i \sqrt{38}-3\text{ or }\lambda =-i \sqrt{38}-3$

```

Insert an error into step 8.
