Given the following problem:

```

Find all the eigenvalues of the matrix $M:$

$M=\left(
\begin{array}{cc}
-1 & -2 \\
 -8 & -6 \\
\end{array}
\right)$

```

And correct solution:

```

1. Find $\lambda \in \mathbb{C}\text{ such that }M v=\lambda  v\text{ for some nonzero vector }v:$

$M v=\lambda  v$

2. Rewrite the equation $M v=\lambda  v\text{ as }(M-\mathbb{I} \lambda ) v=0:$

$(M-\mathbb{I} \lambda ) v=0$

3. Find all $\lambda\text{  such that }| M-\mathbb{I} \lambda | =0:$

$| M-\mathbb{I} \lambda | =0$

4. $\begin{array}{lll}
M-\mathbb{I} \lambda  & = & \left(
\begin{array}{cc}
-1 & -2 \\
 -8 & -6 \\
\end{array}
\right)-\left(
\begin{array}{cc}
1 & 0 \\
 0 & 1 \\
\end{array}
\right) \lambda  \\
\text{  }& = & \left(
\begin{array}{cc}
-\lambda -1 & -2 \\
 -8 & -\lambda -6 \\
\end{array}
\right) \\
\end{array}$

$\left| 
\begin{array}{cc}
-\lambda -1 & -2 \\
 -8 & -\lambda -6 \\
\end{array}
\right| =0$

5. $\left| 
\begin{array}{cc}
-\lambda -1 & -2 \\
 -8 & -\lambda -6 \\
\end{array}
\right| =\lambda ^2+7 \lambda -10:$

$\lambda ^2+7 \lambda -10=0$

6. Add 10 to both sides:

$\lambda ^2+7 \lambda =10$

7. Add $\frac{49}{4}$ to both sides:

$\lambda ^2+7 \lambda +\frac{49}{4}=\frac{89}{4}$

8. Write the left hand side as a square:

$\left(\lambda +\frac{7}{2}\right)^2=\frac{89}{4}$

9. Take the square root of both sides:

$\lambda +\frac{7}{2}=\frac{\sqrt{89}}{2}\text{ or }\lambda +\frac{7}{2}=-\frac{\sqrt{89}}{2}$

10. Subtract $\frac{7}{2}$ from both sides:

$\lambda =\frac{\sqrt{89}}{2}-\frac{7}{2}\text{ or }\lambda +\frac{7}{2}=-\frac{\sqrt{89}}{2}$

11. Subtract $\frac{7}{2}$ from both sides:

Answer: $\lambda =\frac{\sqrt{89}}{2}-\frac{7}{2}\text{ or }\lambda =-\frac{7}{2}-\frac{\sqrt{89}}{2}$

```

Insert an error into step 9.
