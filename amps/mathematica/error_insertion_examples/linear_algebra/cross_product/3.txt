Given the following problem:

```

Compute the following cross product:

$\, (4,6,7)\, \times \, (9,-4,-9)\,$

```

And correct solution:

```

1. Construct a matrix where the first row contains unit vectors $\hat{\text{i}}, \hat{\text{j}},\text{ and }\hat{\text{k}};\text{ and the second and third rows are made of vectors }\, (4,6,7)\,\text{  and }\, (9,-4,-9)\,$ :

$\left(
\begin{array}{ccc}
\hat{\text{i}} & \hat{\text{j}} & \hat{\text{k}} \\
 4 & 6 & 7 \\
 9 & -4 & -9 \\
\end{array}
\right)$

2. Take the determinant of this matrix:

$\left| 
\begin{array}{ccc}
\hat{\text{i}} & \hat{\text{j}} & \hat{\text{k}} \\
 4 & 6 & 7 \\
 9 & -4 & -9 \\
\end{array}
\right|$

3. Expand with respect to row $1:$

= $\left| 
\begin{array}{ccc}
\hat{\text{i}} & \hat{\text{j}} & \hat{\text{k}} \\
 4 & 6 & 7 \\
 9 & -4 & -9 \\
\end{array}
\right|$

4. The determinant of the matrix $\left(
\begin{array}{ccc}
\hat{\text{i}} & \hat{\text{j}} & \hat{\text{k}} \\
 4 & 6 & 7 \\
 9 & -4 & -9 \\
\end{array}
\right)\text{ is given by }\hat{\text{i}} \left| 
\begin{array}{cc}
6 & 7 \\
 -4 & -9 \\
\end{array}
\right| +\left(-\hat{\text{j}}\right) \left| 
\begin{array}{cc}
4 & 7 \\
 9 & -9 \\
\end{array}
\right| +\hat{\text{k}} \left| 
\begin{array}{cc}
4 & 6 \\
 9 & -4 \\
\end{array}
\right|$ :

= $\hat{\text{i}} \left| 
\begin{array}{cc}
6 & 7 \\
 -4 & -9 \\
\end{array}
\right| +\left(-\hat{\text{j}}\right) \left| 
\begin{array}{cc}
4 & 7 \\
 9 & -9 \\
\end{array}
\right| +\hat{\text{k}} \left| 
\begin{array}{cc}
4 & 6 \\
 9 & -4 \\
\end{array}
\right|$

5. $\hat{\text{i}} \left| 
\begin{array}{cc}
6 & 7 \\
 -4 & -9 \\
\end{array}
\right| =\hat{\text{i}} (6 (-9)-7 (-4))=\hat{\text{i}} (-26)=-26 \hat{\text{i}}$:

= $-26 \hat{\text{i}}+\left(-\hat{\text{j}}\right) \left| 
\begin{array}{cc}
4 & 7 \\
 9 & -9 \\
\end{array}
\right| +\hat{\text{k}} \left| 
\begin{array}{cc}
4 & 6 \\
 9 & -4 \\
\end{array}
\right|$

6. $-\hat{\text{j}} \left| 
\begin{array}{cc}
4 & 7 \\
 9 & -9 \\
\end{array}
\right| =-\hat{\text{j}} (4 (-9)-7\ 9)=-\hat{\text{j}} (-99)=99 \hat{\text{j}}$:

= $-26 \hat{\text{i}}+99 \hat{\text{j}}+\hat{\text{k}} \left| 
\begin{array}{cc}
4 & 6 \\
 9 & -4 \\
\end{array}
\right|$

7. $\hat{\text{k}} \left| 
\begin{array}{cc}
4 & 6 \\
 9 & -4 \\
\end{array}
\right| =\hat{\text{k}} (4 (-4)-6\ 9)=\hat{\text{k}} (-70)=-70 \hat{\text{k}}$:

= $-26 \hat{\text{i}}+99 \hat{\text{j}}+-70 \hat{\text{k}}$

8. Order the terms in a more natural way:

= $-26 \hat{\text{i}}+99 \hat{\text{j}}-70 \hat{\text{k}}$

9. $-26 \hat{\text{i}}+99 \hat{\text{j}}-70 \hat{\text{k}}=\, (-26,99,-70)\,$ :

Answer: $\, (-26,99,-70)\,$

```

Insert an error into step 8.
