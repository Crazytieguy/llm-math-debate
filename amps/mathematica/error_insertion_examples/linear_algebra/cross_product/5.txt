Given the following problem:

```

Compute the following cross product:

$\, (-2,7,-9)\, \times \, (-8,1,-6)\,$

```

And correct solution:

```

1. Construct a matrix where the first row contains unit vectors $\hat{\text{i}}, \hat{\text{j}},\text{ and }\hat{\text{k}};\text{ and the second and third rows are made of vectors }\, (-2,7,-9)\,\text{  and }\, (-8,1,-6)\,$ :

$\left(
\begin{array}{ccc}
\hat{\text{i}} & \hat{\text{j}} & \hat{\text{k}} \\
 -2 & 7 & -9 \\
 -8 & 1 & -6 \\
\end{array}
\right)$

2. Take the determinant of this matrix:

$\left| 
\begin{array}{ccc}
\hat{\text{i}} & \hat{\text{j}} & \hat{\text{k}} \\
 -2 & 7 & -9 \\
 -8 & 1 & -6 \\
\end{array}
\right|$

3. Expand with respect to row $1:$

= $\left| 
\begin{array}{ccc}
\hat{\text{i}} & \hat{\text{j}} & \hat{\text{k}} \\
 -2 & 7 & -9 \\
 -8 & 1 & -6 \\
\end{array}
\right|$

4. The determinant of the matrix $\left(
\begin{array}{ccc}
\hat{\text{i}} & \hat{\text{j}} & \hat{\text{k}} \\
 -2 & 7 & -9 \\
 -8 & 1 & -6 \\
\end{array}
\right)\text{ is given by }\hat{\text{i}} \left| 
\begin{array}{cc}
7 & -9 \\
 1 & -6 \\
\end{array}
\right| +\left(-\hat{\text{j}}\right) \left| 
\begin{array}{cc}
-2 & -9 \\
 -8 & -6 \\
\end{array}
\right| +\hat{\text{k}} \left| 
\begin{array}{cc}
-2 & 7 \\
 -8 & 1 \\
\end{array}
\right|$ :

= $\hat{\text{i}} \left| 
\begin{array}{cc}
7 & -9 \\
 1 & -6 \\
\end{array}
\right| +\left(-\hat{\text{j}}\right) \left| 
\begin{array}{cc}
-2 & -9 \\
 -8 & -6 \\
\end{array}
\right| +\hat{\text{k}} \left| 
\begin{array}{cc}
-2 & 7 \\
 -8 & 1 \\
\end{array}
\right|$

5. $\hat{\text{i}} \left| 
\begin{array}{cc}
7 & -9 \\
 1 & -6 \\
\end{array}
\right| =\hat{\text{i}} (7 (-6)-(-9)\, \times \, 1)=\hat{\text{i}} (-33)=-33 \hat{\text{i}}$:

= $-33 \hat{\text{i}}+\left(-\hat{\text{j}}\right) \left| 
\begin{array}{cc}
-2 & -9 \\
 -8 & -6 \\
\end{array}
\right| +\hat{\text{k}} \left| 
\begin{array}{cc}
-2 & 7 \\
 -8 & 1 \\
\end{array}
\right|$

6. $-\hat{\text{j}} \left| 
\begin{array}{cc}
-2 & -9 \\
 -8 & -6 \\
\end{array}
\right| =-\hat{\text{j}} ((-2)\, (-6)-(-9)\, (-8))=-\hat{\text{j}} (-60)=60 \hat{\text{j}}$:

= $-33 \hat{\text{i}}+60 \hat{\text{j}}+\hat{\text{k}} \left| 
\begin{array}{cc}
-2 & 7 \\
 -8 & 1 \\
\end{array}
\right|$

7. $\hat{\text{k}} \left| 
\begin{array}{cc}
-2 & 7 \\
 -8 & 1 \\
\end{array}
\right| =\hat{\text{k}} ((-2)\, \times \, 1-7 (-8))=\hat{\text{k}} 54=54 \hat{\text{k}}$:

= $-33 \hat{\text{i}}+60 \hat{\text{j}}+54 \hat{\text{k}}$

8. Order the terms in a more natural way:

= $-33 \hat{\text{i}}+60 \hat{\text{j}}+54 \hat{\text{k}}$

9. $-33 \hat{\text{i}}+60 \hat{\text{j}}+54 \hat{\text{k}}=\, (-33,60,54)\,$ :

Answer: $\, (-33,60,54)\,$

```
