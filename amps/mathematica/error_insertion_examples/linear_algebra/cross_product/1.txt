Given the following problem:

```

Compute the following cross product:

$\, (-7,-7,9)\, \times \, (3,1,-7)\,$

```

And correct solution:

```

1. Construct a matrix where the first row contains unit vectors $\hat{\text{i}}, \hat{\text{j}},\text{ and }\hat{\text{k}};\text{ and the second and third rows are made of vectors }\, (-7,-7,9)\,\text{  and }\, (3,1,-7)\,$ :

$\left(
\begin{array}{ccc}
\hat{\text{i}} & \hat{\text{j}} & \hat{\text{k}} \\
 -7 & -7 & 9 \\
 3 & 1 & -7 \\
\end{array}
\right)$

2. Take the determinant of this matrix:

$\left| 
\begin{array}{ccc}
\hat{\text{i}} & \hat{\text{j}} & \hat{\text{k}} \\
 -7 & -7 & 9 \\
 3 & 1 & -7 \\
\end{array}
\right|$

3. Expand with respect to row $1:$

= $\left| 
\begin{array}{ccc}
\hat{\text{i}} & \hat{\text{j}} & \hat{\text{k}} \\
 -7 & -7 & 9 \\
 3 & 1 & -7 \\
\end{array}
\right|$

4. The determinant of the matrix $\left(
\begin{array}{ccc}
\hat{\text{i}} & \hat{\text{j}} & \hat{\text{k}} \\
 -7 & -7 & 9 \\
 3 & 1 & -7 \\
\end{array}
\right)\text{ is given by }\hat{\text{i}} \left| 
\begin{array}{cc}
-7 & 9 \\
 1 & -7 \\
\end{array}
\right| +\left(-\hat{\text{j}}\right) \left| 
\begin{array}{cc}
-7 & 9 \\
 3 & -7 \\
\end{array}
\right| +\hat{\text{k}} \left| 
\begin{array}{cc}
-7 & -7 \\
 3 & 1 \\
\end{array}
\right|$ :

= $\hat{\text{i}} \left| 
\begin{array}{cc}
-7 & 9 \\
 1 & -7 \\
\end{array}
\right| +\left(-\hat{\text{j}}\right) \left| 
\begin{array}{cc}
-7 & 9 \\
 3 & -7 \\
\end{array}
\right| +\hat{\text{k}} \left| 
\begin{array}{cc}
-7 & -7 \\
 3 & 1 \\
\end{array}
\right|$

5. $\hat{\text{i}} \left| 
\begin{array}{cc}
-7 & 9 \\
 1 & -7 \\
\end{array}
\right| =\hat{\text{i}} ((-7)\, (-7)-9\ 1)=\hat{\text{i}} 40=40 \hat{\text{i}}$:

= $40 \hat{\text{i}}+\left(-\hat{\text{j}}\right) \left| 
\begin{array}{cc}
-7 & 9 \\
 3 & -7 \\
\end{array}
\right| +\hat{\text{k}} \left| 
\begin{array}{cc}
-7 & -7 \\
 3 & 1 \\
\end{array}
\right|$

6. $-\hat{\text{j}} \left| 
\begin{array}{cc}
-7 & 9 \\
 3 & -7 \\
\end{array}
\right| =-\hat{\text{j}} ((-7)\, (-7)-9\ 3)=-\hat{\text{j}} 22=-22 \hat{\text{j}}$:

= $40 \hat{\text{i}}+-22 \hat{\text{j}}+\hat{\text{k}} \left| 
\begin{array}{cc}
-7 & -7 \\
 3 & 1 \\
\end{array}
\right|$

7. $\hat{\text{k}} \left| 
\begin{array}{cc}
-7 & -7 \\
 3 & 1 \\
\end{array}
\right| =\hat{\text{k}} ((-7)\, \times \, 1-(-7)\, \times \, 3)=\hat{\text{k}} 14=14 \hat{\text{k}}$:

= $40 \hat{\text{i}}-22 \hat{\text{j}}+14 \hat{\text{k}}$

8. Order the terms in a more natural way:

= $40 \hat{\text{i}}-22 \hat{\text{j}}+14 \hat{\text{k}}$

9. $40 \hat{\text{i}}-22 \hat{\text{j}}+14 \hat{\text{k}}=\, (40,-22,14)\,$ :

Answer: $\, (40,-22,14)\,$

```
