Given the following problem:

```

Compute the following cross product:

$\, (3,6,-3)\, \times \, (1,9,-7)\,$

```

And correct solution:

```

1. Construct a matrix where the first row contains unit vectors $\hat{\text{i}}, \hat{\text{j}},\text{ and }\hat{\text{k}};\text{ and the second and third rows are made of vectors }\, (3,6,-3)\,\text{  and }\, (1,9,-7)\,$ :

$\left(
\begin{array}{ccc}
\hat{\text{i}} & \hat{\text{j}} & \hat{\text{k}} \\
 3 & 6 & -3 \\
 1 & 9 & -7 \\
\end{array}
\right)$

2. Take the determinant of this matrix:

$\left| 
\begin{array}{ccc}
\hat{\text{i}} & \hat{\text{j}} & \hat{\text{k}} \\
 3 & 6 & -3 \\
 1 & 9 & -7 \\
\end{array}
\right|$

3. Expand with respect to row $1:$

= $\left| 
\begin{array}{ccc}
\hat{\text{i}} & \hat{\text{j}} & \hat{\text{k}} \\
 3 & 6 & -3 \\
 1 & 9 & -7 \\
\end{array}
\right|$

4. The determinant of the matrix $\left(
\begin{array}{ccc}
\hat{\text{i}} & \hat{\text{j}} & \hat{\text{k}} \\
 3 & 6 & -3 \\
 1 & 9 & -7 \\
\end{array}
\right)\text{ is given by }\hat{\text{i}} \left| 
\begin{array}{cc}
6 & -3 \\
 9 & -7 \\
\end{array}
\right| +\left(-\hat{\text{j}}\right) \left| 
\begin{array}{cc}
3 & -3 \\
 1 & -7 \\
\end{array}
\right| +\hat{\text{k}} \left| 
\begin{array}{cc}
3 & 6 \\
 1 & 9 \\
\end{array}
\right|$ :

= $\hat{\text{i}} \left| 
\begin{array}{cc}
6 & -3 \\
 9 & -7 \\
\end{array}
\right| +\left(-\hat{\text{j}}\right) \left| 
\begin{array}{cc}
3 & -3 \\
 1 & -7 \\
\end{array}
\right| +\hat{\text{k}} \left| 
\begin{array}{cc}
3 & 6 \\
 1 & 9 \\
\end{array}
\right|$

5. $\hat{\text{i}} \left| 
\begin{array}{cc}
6 & -3 \\
 9 & -7 \\
\end{array}
\right| =\hat{\text{i}} (6 (-7)-(-3)\, \times \, 9)=\hat{\text{i}} (-15)=-15 \hat{\text{i}}$:

= $-15 \hat{\text{i}}+\left(-\hat{\text{j}}\right) \left| 
\begin{array}{cc}
3 & -3 \\
 1 & -7 \\
\end{array}
\right| +\hat{\text{k}} \left| 
\begin{array}{cc}
3 & 6 \\
 1 & 9 \\
\end{array}
\right|$

6. $-\hat{\text{j}} \left| 
\begin{array}{cc}
3 & -3 \\
 1 & -7 \\
\end{array}
\right| =-\hat{\text{j}} (3 (-7)-(-3)\, \times \, 1)=-\hat{\text{j}} (-18)=18 \hat{\text{j}}$:

= $-15 \hat{\text{i}}+18 \hat{\text{j}}+\hat{\text{k}} \left| 
\begin{array}{cc}
3 & 6 \\
 1 & 9 \\
\end{array}
\right|$

7. $\hat{\text{k}} \left| 
\begin{array}{cc}
3 & 6 \\
 1 & 9 \\
\end{array}
\right| =\hat{\text{k}} (3\ 9-6\ 1)=\hat{\text{k}} 21=21 \hat{\text{k}}$:

= $-15 \hat{\text{i}}+18 \hat{\text{j}}+21 \hat{\text{k}}$

8. Order the terms in a more natural way:

= $-15 \hat{\text{i}}+18 \hat{\text{j}}+21 \hat{\text{k}}$

9. $-15 \hat{\text{i}}+18 \hat{\text{j}}+21 \hat{\text{k}}=\, (-15,18,21)\,$ :

Answer: $\, (-15,18,21)\,$

```

Insert an error into step 2.
