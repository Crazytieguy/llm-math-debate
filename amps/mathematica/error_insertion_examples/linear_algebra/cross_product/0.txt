Given the following problem:

```

Compute the following cross product:

$\, (-8,-3,6)\, \times \, (-3,5,2)\,$

```

And correct solution:

```

1. Construct a matrix where the first row contains unit vectors $\hat{\text{i}}, \hat{\text{j}},\text{ and }\hat{\text{k}};\text{ and the second and third rows are made of vectors }\, (-8,-3,6)\,\text{  and }\, (-3,5,2)\,$ :

$\left(
\begin{array}{ccc}
\hat{\text{i}} & \hat{\text{j}} & \hat{\text{k}} \\
 -8 & -3 & 6 \\
 -3 & 5 & 2 \\
\end{array}
\right)$

2. Take the determinant of this matrix:

$\left| 
\begin{array}{ccc}
\hat{\text{i}} & \hat{\text{j}} & \hat{\text{k}} \\
 -8 & -3 & 6 \\
 -3 & 5 & 2 \\
\end{array}
\right|$

3. Expand with respect to row $1:$

= $\left| 
\begin{array}{ccc}
\hat{\text{i}} & \hat{\text{j}} & \hat{\text{k}} \\
 -8 & -3 & 6 \\
 -3 & 5 & 2 \\
\end{array}
\right|$

4. The determinant of the matrix $\left(
\begin{array}{ccc}
\hat{\text{i}} & \hat{\text{j}} & \hat{\text{k}} \\
 -8 & -3 & 6 \\
 -3 & 5 & 2 \\
\end{array}
\right)\text{ is given by }\hat{\text{i}} \left| 
\begin{array}{cc}
-3 & 6 \\
 5 & 2 \\
\end{array}
\right| +\left(-\hat{\text{j}}\right) \left| 
\begin{array}{cc}
-8 & 6 \\
 -3 & 2 \\
\end{array}
\right| +\hat{\text{k}} \left| 
\begin{array}{cc}
-8 & -3 \\
 -3 & 5 \\
\end{array}
\right|$ :

= $\hat{\text{i}} \left| 
\begin{array}{cc}
-3 & 6 \\
 5 & 2 \\
\end{array}
\right| +\left(-\hat{\text{j}}\right) \left| 
\begin{array}{cc}
-8 & 6 \\
 -3 & 2 \\
\end{array}
\right| +\hat{\text{k}} \left| 
\begin{array}{cc}
-8 & -3 \\
 -3 & 5 \\
\end{array}
\right|$

5. $\hat{\text{i}} \left| 
\begin{array}{cc}
-3 & 6 \\
 5 & 2 \\
\end{array}
\right| =\hat{\text{i}} ((-3)\, \times \, 2-6\ 5)=\hat{\text{i}} (-36)=-36 \hat{\text{i}}$:

= $-36 \hat{\text{i}}+\left(-\hat{\text{j}}\right) \left| 
\begin{array}{cc}
-8 & 6 \\
 -3 & 2 \\
\end{array}
\right| +\hat{\text{k}} \left| 
\begin{array}{cc}
-8 & -3 \\
 -3 & 5 \\
\end{array}
\right|$

6. $-\hat{\text{j}} \left| 
\begin{array}{cc}
-8 & 6 \\
 -3 & 2 \\
\end{array}
\right| =-\hat{\text{j}} ((-8)\, \times \, 2-6 (-3))=-\hat{\text{j}} 2=-2 \hat{\text{j}}$:

= $-36 \hat{\text{i}}+-2 \hat{\text{j}}+\hat{\text{k}} \left| 
\begin{array}{cc}
-8 & -3 \\
 -3 & 5 \\
\end{array}
\right|$

7. $\hat{\text{k}} \left| 
\begin{array}{cc}
-8 & -3 \\
 -3 & 5 \\
\end{array}
\right| =\hat{\text{k}} ((-8)\, \times \, 5-(-3)\, (-3))=\hat{\text{k}} (-49)=-49 \hat{\text{k}}$:

= $-36 \hat{\text{i}}-2 \hat{\text{j}}+-49 \hat{\text{k}}$

8. Order the terms in a more natural way:

= $-36 \hat{\text{i}}-2 \hat{\text{j}}-49 \hat{\text{k}}$

9. $-36 \hat{\text{i}}-2 \hat{\text{j}}-49 \hat{\text{k}}=\, (-36,-2,-49)\,$ :

Answer: $\, (-36,-2,-49)\,$

```
