Given the following problem:

```

Compute the following cross product:

$\, (-10,5,-9)\, \times \, (-7,-6,-3)\,$

```

And correct solution:

```

1. Construct a matrix where the first row contains unit vectors $\hat{\text{i}}, \hat{\text{j}},\text{ and }\hat{\text{k}};\text{ and the second and third rows are made of vectors }\, (-10,5,-9)\,\text{  and }\, (-7,-6,-3)\,$ :

$\left(
\begin{array}{ccc}
\hat{\text{i}} & \hat{\text{j}} & \hat{\text{k}} \\
 -10 & 5 & -9 \\
 -7 & -6 & -3 \\
\end{array}
\right)$

2. Take the determinant of this matrix:

$\left| 
\begin{array}{ccc}
\hat{\text{i}} & \hat{\text{j}} & \hat{\text{k}} \\
 -10 & 5 & -9 \\
 -7 & -6 & -3 \\
\end{array}
\right|$

3. Expand with respect to row $1:$

= $\left| 
\begin{array}{ccc}
\hat{\text{i}} & \hat{\text{j}} & \hat{\text{k}} \\
 -10 & 5 & -9 \\
 -7 & -6 & -3 \\
\end{array}
\right|$

4. The determinant of the matrix $\left(
\begin{array}{ccc}
\hat{\text{i}} & \hat{\text{j}} & \hat{\text{k}} \\
 -10 & 5 & -9 \\
 -7 & -6 & -3 \\
\end{array}
\right)\text{ is given by }\hat{\text{i}} \left| 
\begin{array}{cc}
5 & -9 \\
 -6 & -3 \\
\end{array}
\right| +\left(-\hat{\text{j}}\right) \left| 
\begin{array}{cc}
-10 & -9 \\
 -7 & -3 \\
\end{array}
\right| +\hat{\text{k}} \left| 
\begin{array}{cc}
-10 & 5 \\
 -7 & -6 \\
\end{array}
\right|$ :

= $\hat{\text{i}} \left| 
\begin{array}{cc}
5 & -9 \\
 -6 & -3 \\
\end{array}
\right| +\left(-\hat{\text{j}}\right) \left| 
\begin{array}{cc}
-10 & -9 \\
 -7 & -3 \\
\end{array}
\right| +\hat{\text{k}} \left| 
\begin{array}{cc}
-10 & 5 \\
 -7 & -6 \\
\end{array}
\right|$

5. $\hat{\text{i}} \left| 
\begin{array}{cc}
5 & -9 \\
 -6 & -3 \\
\end{array}
\right| =\hat{\text{i}} (5 (-3)-(-9)\, (-6))=\hat{\text{i}} (-69)=-69 \hat{\text{i}}$:

= $-69 \hat{\text{i}}+\left(-\hat{\text{j}}\right) \left| 
\begin{array}{cc}
-10 & -9 \\
 -7 & -3 \\
\end{array}
\right| +\hat{\text{k}} \left| 
\begin{array}{cc}
-10 & 5 \\
 -7 & -6 \\
\end{array}
\right|$

6. $-\hat{\text{j}} \left| 
\begin{array}{cc}
-10 & -9 \\
 -7 & -3 \\
\end{array}
\right| =-\hat{\text{j}} ((-10)\, (-3)-(-9)\, (-7))=-\hat{\text{j}} (-33)=33 \hat{\text{j}}$:

= $-69 \hat{\text{i}}+33 \hat{\text{j}}+\hat{\text{k}} \left| 
\begin{array}{cc}
-10 & 5 \\
 -7 & -6 \\
\end{array}
\right|$

7. $\hat{\text{k}} \left| 
\begin{array}{cc}
-10 & 5 \\
 -7 & -6 \\
\end{array}
\right| =\hat{\text{k}} ((-10)\, (-6)-5 (-7))=\hat{\text{k}} 95=95 \hat{\text{k}}$:

= $-69 \hat{\text{i}}+33 \hat{\text{j}}+95 \hat{\text{k}}$

8. Order the terms in a more natural way:

= $-69 \hat{\text{i}}+33 \hat{\text{j}}+95 \hat{\text{k}}$

9. $-69 \hat{\text{i}}+33 \hat{\text{j}}+95 \hat{\text{k}}=\, (-69,33,95)\,$ :

Answer: $\, (-69,33,95)\,$

```
