Explanation: $\sqrt{4}$ can be simplified further to $2$.

Note: Make sure to carry the error onto the final answer, replacing $2$ with $\sqrt{4}$.

```

1. $\sqrt{80} = \sqrt{2^4\times 5} = 2^2 \sqrt{5}$:

$2^2 \sqrt{5}+\sqrt{7}+\sqrt{44}$

2. $2^2=4:$

$4 \sqrt{5}+\sqrt{7}+\sqrt{44}$

3. $\sqrt{44} = \sqrt{4\times 11} = \sqrt{4} \sqrt{11}$:

Answer: $4 \sqrt{5}+\sqrt{7}+\sqrt{4} \sqrt{11}$
```
