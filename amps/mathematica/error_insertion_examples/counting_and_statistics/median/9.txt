Given the following problem:

```

Find the median of the list:

$\left(7 \log (2),8,-2 \sqrt{3},-1\right)$

```

And correct solution:

```

1. The list, sorted from smallest to largest, is:

$\left(-2 \sqrt{3},-1,7 \log (2),8\right)$

2. The two elements in the middle of the list $\left(-2 \sqrt{3},-1,7 \log (2),8\right)\text{ are }-1\text{ and }7 \log (2):$

$\left(-2 \sqrt{3},-1,7 \log (2),8\right)$

3. The median is the average of the two middle elements, $-1\text{ and }7 \log (2).$ This average is:

Answer: $\frac{7 \log (2)-1}{2}$

```

Insert an error into step 1.
