Given the following problem:

```

Find the median of the list:

$\left(-7,-\frac{8}{\pi },3,\frac{14}{\sqrt{\pi }}\right)$

```

And correct solution:

```

1. The two elements in the middle of the list $\left(-7,-\frac{8}{\pi },3,\frac{14}{\sqrt{\pi }}\right)\text{ are }-\frac{8}{\pi }\text{ and }3:$

$\left(-7,-\frac{8}{\pi },3,14 \pi ^{-1/2}\right)$

2. The median is the average of the two middle elements, $-\frac{8}{\pi }\text{ and }3.$ This average is:

Answer: $\frac{3-\frac{8}{\pi }}{2} = \frac{3-\frac{8}{\pi }}{2}$

```
