Given the following problem:

```

Find the geometric mean of the list:

$(100,8,27,216,10,5)$

```

And correct solution:

```

1. The geometric mean of a list of numbers is given by:

$(\text{product of elements})^{\frac{1}{(\text{number of elements})}}$

2. $(\text{product of elements}) =\text{ 100 $\times $ }8\text{ $\times $ 27 $\times $ 216 $\times $ 10 $\times $ }5:$

$\sqrt[(\text{number of elements})]{(\text{100 $\times $ }8\text{ $\times $ 27 $\times $ 216 $\times $ 10 $\times $ }5)}$

3. Counting, we see that the list has $6$ elements:

$(\text{100 $\times $ }8\text{ $\times $ 27 $\times $ 216 $\times $ 10 $\times $ }5)^{\frac{1}{6}} = \sqrt[6]{\text{100 $\times $ }8\text{ $\times $ 27 $\times $ 216 $\times $ 10 $\times $ }5}$

4. 100 $\times $ $8\text{ $\times $ 27 $\times $ 216 $\times $ 10 $\times $ }5=233280000:$

$\sqrt[6]{233280000}$

5. $\sqrt[6]{233280000} = \sqrt[6]{2^9\times 3^6\times 5^4} = 2\times 3 \sqrt{2}\times 5^{2/3}$:

$2\times 3 \sqrt{2}\times 5^{2/3}$

6. $2\times 3 = 6:$

Answer: $6 \sqrt{2}\times 5^{2/3}$

```

Insert an error into step 2.
