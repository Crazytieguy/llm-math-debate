Given the following problem:

```

Find the geometric mean of the list:

$(27,16,4,25,512,16)$

```

And correct solution:

```

1. The geometric mean of a list of numbers is given by:

$(\text{product of elements})^{\frac{1}{(\text{number of elements})}}$

2. $(\text{product of elements}) =\text{ 27 $\times $ 16 $\times $ }4\text{ $\times $ 25 $\times $ 512 $\times $ }16:$

$\sqrt[(\text{number of elements})]{(\text{27 $\times $ 16 $\times $ }4\text{ $\times $ 25 $\times $ 512 $\times $ }16)}$

3. Counting, we see that the list has $6$ elements:

$(\text{27 $\times $ 16 $\times $ }4\text{ $\times $ 25 $\times $ 512 $\times $ }16)^{\frac{1}{6}} = \sqrt[6]{\text{27 $\times $ 16 $\times $ }4\text{ $\times $ 25 $\times $ 512 $\times $ }16}$

4. 27 $\times $ 16 $\times $ $4\text{ $\times $ 25 $\times $ 512 $\times $ }16=353894400:$

$\sqrt[6]{353894400}$

5. $\sqrt[6]{353894400} = \sqrt[6]{2^{19}\times 3^3\times 5^2} = 2^3 \sqrt[6]{2} \sqrt{3} \sqrt[3]{5}$:

$2^3 \sqrt[6]{2} \sqrt{3} \sqrt[3]{5}$

6. $2^3=2\times 2^2:$

$2\times 2^2 \sqrt[6]{2} \sqrt{3} \sqrt[3]{5}$

7. $2^2=4:$

$2\times 4 \sqrt[6]{2} \sqrt{3} \sqrt[3]{5}$

8. $2\times 4 = 8:$

Answer: $8 \sqrt[6]{2} \sqrt{3} \sqrt[3]{5}$

```

Insert an error into step 2.
