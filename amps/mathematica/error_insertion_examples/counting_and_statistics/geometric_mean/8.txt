Given the following problem:

```

Find the geometric mean of the list:

$(10,49,2,16,1,343)$

```

And correct solution:

```

1. The geometric mean of a list of numbers is given by:

$(\text{product of elements})^{\frac{1}{(\text{number of elements})}}$

2. $(\text{product of elements}) =\text{ 10 $\times $ 49 $\times $ }2\text{ $\times $ 16 $\times $ }1\text{ $\times $ }343:$

$\sqrt[(\text{number of elements})]{(\text{10 $\times $ 49 $\times $ }2\text{ $\times $ 16 $\times $ }1\text{ $\times $ }343)}$

3. Counting, we see that the list has $6$ elements:

$(\text{10 $\times $ 49 $\times $ }2\text{ $\times $ 16 $\times $ }1\text{ $\times $ }343)^{\frac{1}{6}} = \sqrt[6]{\text{10 $\times $ 49 $\times $ }2\text{ $\times $ 16 $\times $ }1\text{ $\times $ }343}$

4. 10 $\times $ 49 $\times $ $2\text{ $\times $ 16 $\times $ }1\text{ $\times $ }343=5378240:$

$\sqrt[6]{5378240}$

5. $\sqrt[6]{5378240} = \sqrt[6]{2^6\times 5\times 7^5} = 2 \sqrt[6]{5}\times 7^{5/6}$:

Answer: $2 \sqrt[6]{5}\times 7^{5/6}$

```

Insert an error into step 4.
