Given the following problem:

```

Find the geometric mean of the list:

$(125,8,9)$

```

And correct solution:

```

1. The geometric mean of a list of numbers is given by:

$(\text{product of elements})^{\frac{1}{(\text{number of elements})}}$

2. $(\text{product of elements}) =\text{ 125 $\times $ }8\text{ $\times $ }9:$

$\sqrt[(\text{number of elements})]{(\text{125 $\times $ }8\text{ $\times $ }9)}$

3. Counting, we see that the list has $3$ elements:

$(\text{125 $\times $ }8\text{ $\times $ }9)^{\frac{1}{3}} = \sqrt[3]{\text{125 $\times $ }8\text{ $\times $ }9}$

4. 125 $\times $ $8\text{ $\times $ }9=9000:$

$\sqrt[3]{9000}$

5. $\sqrt[3]{9000} = \sqrt[3]{3^2\times 10^3} = 10\times 3^{2/3}$:

Answer: $10\times 3^{2/3}$

```

Insert an error into step 5.
