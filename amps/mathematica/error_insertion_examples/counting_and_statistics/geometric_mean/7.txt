Given the following problem:

```

Find the geometric mean of the list:

$(36,36,6,64,8)$

```

And correct solution:

```

1. The geometric mean of a list of numbers is given by:

$(\text{product of elements})^{\frac{1}{(\text{number of elements})}}$

2. $(\text{product of elements}) =\text{ 36 $\times $ 36 $\times $ }6\text{ $\times $ 64 $\times $ }8:$

$\sqrt[(\text{number of elements})]{(\text{36 $\times $ 36 $\times $ }6\text{ $\times $ 64 $\times $ }8)}$

3. Counting, we see that the list has $5$ elements:

$(\text{36 $\times $ 36 $\times $ }6\text{ $\times $ 64 $\times $ }8)^{\frac{1}{5}} = \sqrt[5]{\text{36 $\times $ 36 $\times $ }6\text{ $\times $ 64 $\times $ }8}$

4. 36 $\times $ 36 $\times $ $6\text{ $\times $ 64 $\times $ }8=3981312:$

$\sqrt[5]{3981312}$

5. $\sqrt[5]{3981312} = \sqrt[5]{2^{14}\times 3^5} = 2^2\times 3\times 2^{4/5}$:

$2^2\times 3\times 2^{4/5}$

6. $2^2=4:$

$4\times 3\times 2^{4/5}$

7. $4\times 3 = 12:$

Answer: $12\times 2^{4/5}$

```

Insert an error into step 3.
