Given the following problem:

```

Find the geometric mean of the list:

$(125,9,8,10,216,27)$

```

And correct solution:

```

1. The geometric mean of a list of numbers is given by:

$(\text{product of elements})^{\frac{1}{(\text{number of elements})}}$

2. $(\text{product of elements}) =\text{ 125 $\times $ }9\text{ $\times $ }8\text{ $\times $ 10 $\times $ 216 $\times $ }27:$

$\sqrt[(\text{number of elements})]{(\text{125 $\times $ }9\text{ $\times $ }8\text{ $\times $ 10 $\times $ 216 $\times $ }27)}$

3. Counting, we see that the list has $6$ elements:

$(\text{125 $\times $ }9\text{ $\times $ }8\text{ $\times $ 10 $\times $ 216 $\times $ }27)^{\frac{1}{6}} = \sqrt[6]{\text{125 $\times $ }9\text{ $\times $ }8\text{ $\times $ 10 $\times $ 216 $\times $ }27}$

4. 125 $\times $ $9\text{ $\times $ }8\text{ $\times $ 10 $\times $ 216 $\times $ }27=524880000:$

$\sqrt[6]{524880000}$

5. $\sqrt[6]{524880000} = \sqrt[6]{2^7\times 3^8\times 5^4} = 2\times 3 \sqrt[6]{2} \sqrt[3]{3}\times 5^{2/3}$:

$2\times 3 \sqrt[6]{2} \sqrt[3]{3}\times 5^{2/3}$

6. $2\times 3 = 6:$

Answer: $6 \sqrt[6]{2} \sqrt[3]{3}\times 5^{2/3}$

```

Insert an error into step 2.
