Given the following problem:

```

Find the mean of the list:

$(-4 e,-2 e,e,-e,-2 e,-3 e,-2 e,e)$

```

And correct solution:

```

1. The mean of a list of numbers is given by:

$\frac{(\text{sum of elements})}{(\text{number of elements})}$

2. $(\text{sum of elements}) = -4 e-2 e+e-e-2 e-3 e-2 e+e:$

$\frac{-4 e-2 e+e-e-2 e-3 e-2 e+e}{(\text{number of elements})}$

3. Counting, we see that the list has $8$ elements:

$\frac{-4 e-2 e+e-e-2 e-3 e-2 e+e}{8}$

4. $-4 e-2 e+e-e-2 e-3 e-2 e+e = -12 e:$

$\frac{1}{8} (-12 e)$

5. $\frac{-12 e}{8}=-\frac{3 e}{2}$:

Answer: $-\frac{3 e}{2}$

```
