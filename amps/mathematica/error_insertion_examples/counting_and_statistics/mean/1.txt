Given the following problem:

```

Find the mean of the list:

$(-3 e,3 e,-3 e,e,0,-2 e,3 e,-4 e)$

```

And correct solution:

```

1. The mean of a list of numbers is given by:

$\frac{(\text{sum of elements})}{(\text{number of elements})}$

2. $(\text{sum of elements}) = -3 e+3 e-3 e+e+0-2 e+3 e-4 e:$

$\frac{-3 e+3 e-3 e+e+0-2 e+3 e-4 e}{(\text{number of elements})}$

3. Counting, we see that the list has $8$ elements:

$\frac{-3 e+3 e-3 e+e+0-2 e+3 e-4 e}{8}$

4. $-3 e+3 e-3 e+e+0-2 e+3 e-4 e = -5 e:$

Answer: $\frac{1}{8} (-5 e)$

```
