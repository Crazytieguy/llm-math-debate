Given the following problem:

```

Find the mean of the list:

$(1,9,-1,-10,-2)$

```

And correct solution:

```

1. The mean of a list of numbers is given by:

$\frac{(\text{sum of elements})}{(\text{number of elements})}$

2. $(\text{sum of elements}) = 1+9-1-10-2:$

$\frac{1+9-1-10-2}{(\text{number of elements})}$

3. Counting, we see that the list has $5$ elements:

$\frac{1+9-1-10-2}{5}$

4. $1+9-1-10-2 = -3:$

$-\frac{3}{5}\approx -0.6$

5. $\frac{-3}{5}=-\frac{3}{5}$:

Answer: $-\frac{3}{5}\approx -0.6$

```

Insert an error into step 1.
