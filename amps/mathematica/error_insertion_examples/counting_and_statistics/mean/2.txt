Given the following problem:

```

Find the mean of the list:

$\left(-4 \sqrt{2},-6 \sqrt{2},-2 \sqrt{2}\right)$

```

And correct solution:

```

1. The mean of a list of numbers is given by:

$\frac{(\text{sum of elements})}{(\text{number of elements})}$

2. $(\text{sum of elements}) = -4 \sqrt{2}-6 \sqrt{2}-2 \sqrt{2}$:

$\frac{-4 \sqrt{2}-6 \sqrt{2}-2 \sqrt{2}}{(\text{number of elements})}$

3. Counting, we see that the list has $3$ elements:

$\frac{-4 \sqrt{2}-6 \sqrt{2}-2 \sqrt{2}}{3}$

4. $-4 \sqrt{2}-6 \sqrt{2}-2 \sqrt{2} = -12 \sqrt{2}$:

$\frac{1}{3} \left(-12 \sqrt{2}\right)$

5. $\frac{-12 \sqrt{2}}{3}=-4 \sqrt{2}$:

Answer: $-4 \sqrt{2}$

```
